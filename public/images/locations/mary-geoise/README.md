## Visual Themes

### Duality and Contrast
Mary Geoise is defined by stark contrasts:
- **Beauty vs. Cruelty:** Pristine architecture built on slavery
- **Light vs. Dark:** Bright public areas vs. dark slave pens
- **Facade vs. Reality:** Empty Throne (symbol of equality) vs. Im sitting on it (dictatorship)
- **Heaven vs. Hell:** Literally elevated above the world, but morally the lowest place

### Symbolism
- **Height/Elevation:** Physical position atop Red Line represents claimed superiority
- **White/Gold:** Divine imagery, purity claims (false purity)
- **Empty Throne:** The world's greatest lie - equality claim hiding dictatorship
- **Twenty Swords:** Founding kingdoms (nineteen present, one refused)
- **Bubble Helmets:** Celestial Dragons' refusal to breathe same air as "commoners"
- **Slave Brands:** Hoof of Soaring Dragon - permanent mark of ownership

## Architectural Style

Mary Geoise blends:
- **Ancient Greek/Roman:** Columns, marble, classical proportions
- **Futuristic Elements:** Travelators, advanced security, clean lines
- **Religious Imagery:** Divine aesthetics, heaven-like elevation
- **Fortress Design:** Impregnable, defensive, exclusive

Everything is pristine, white, and golden - creating a "heavenly" appearance that masks the hell of slavery and oppression beneath.

## Notes for Image Selection

The location images should convey:

1. **Scale and Grandeur:** Mary Geoise is ENORMOUS and impressive
2. **Pristine Perfection:** Everything appears flawless (hiding corruption)
3. **Duality:** Beauty built on cruelty, light hiding darkness
4. **Power:** Absolute authority and ancient control
5. **Symbolism:** Empty Throne, twenty swords, elevation above world
6. **History:** Fisher Tiger's raid, Reverie gatherings, ancient secrets

This is the center of the One Piece world's power structure and its greatest corruption. The images should reflect both its imposing grandeur and its moral emptiness.

The Holy Land is a lie - beautiful on the surface, rotten at the core. Images should hint at this truth.
